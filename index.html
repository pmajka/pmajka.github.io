Research interests

  • Integration of multimodal and multiscale neuroanatomical image data. Application of statistical and machine learning methods to get insight from large–scale imaging datasets:
    • Mapping tracer injection studies and construction of a multimodal digital brain atlas of the marmoset monkey cerebral cortex.
  • Biomedical image acquisition and processing, registration and reconstruction of 3D brain images based on serial sections:
    • Development of a open framework for three-dimensional reconstruction of brain images from serial sections - the Possum software (see the publication).
    • Creating multimodal stereotactic atlas of the Monodelphis domestica opossum brain (MRI and microCT, blockface images, Nissl and myelin histology). See: PhD thesis (in polish), YouTube videos: (1, 2), conference posters (1, 2, 3) and abstracts (1, 2, 3, 4).
  • Digital (including three-dimensional) brain atlases construction and dissemination:
    • Membership in the International Neuroinformatics Coordinating Facility Program on Digital Brain Atlasing.
    • Developing a generic data format to store and distribute 2D atlas delineations and designing 3D Brain Atlas Reconstructor software.
    • Creating the 3dBAR web service, an online repository of three-dimensional models of brain structures (23 atlas datasets, 120 registered users as of March 2016). See: publication, video tutorials, API reference.
